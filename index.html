Project Work

Web-Based Information Management System for Lingkod Maria Community Development and Advocacy Center

Project Task/Challenge: The Lingkod Maria Community Development and Advocacy Center needed a robust and accessible system to manage their community data, including beneficiary information, program details, and outreach activities. The existing manual processes were inefficient, prone to errors, and made data retrieval and reporting challenging. The primary challenge was to centralize this information and make it easily manageable for their staff.

Goals/Objectives: The main goals were to develop a web-based system that would:

  • Streamline data entry and retrieval for community members and programs.
  • Improve reporting capabilities for internal analysis and external stakeholders.
  • Enhance data security and integrity.
  • Provide an intuitive user interface for non-technical staff.

My Involvement: As a key member of the development team, I was primarily responsible for the back-end development, including database design (MySQL) and API development using a PHP-based framework. I also contributed to the front-end integration, ensuring seamless interaction between the user interface and the database. My role involved translating functional requirements into technical specifications and implementing robust solutions.

Approach: We adopted an Agile development methodology, with iterative sprints to deliver features incrementally. My approach involved:

  • Requirement Analysis: Collaborating closely with the center's staff to understand their needs and workflows.
  • Database Design: Creating a normalized and efficient database schema to store various types of information.
  • API Development: Building secure and efficient APIs for data manipulation and retrieval.
  • Testing: Conducting thorough unit and integration testing to ensure data accuracy and system reliability.
  • User Training: Assisting in training the center's staff on how to effectively use the new system.

Outcomes: The developed system significantly improved the center's operational efficiency. Data management became centralized and accessible, reducing manual errors and saving considerable time. Reporting capabilities were enhanced, allowing the center to generate insights into their programs more effectively and demonstrate their impact to donors and partners. The intuitive interface led to quick adoption by staff, and the overall system provided a solid foundation for future expansions.

Developed a Standardized Grading System for Text Content using Large Language Models

Project Task/Challenge: The challenge was to create an automated and consistent method for grading large volumes of text content, such as essays or reports, which traditionally relied on subjective manual assessment. The goal was to leverage Large Language Models (LLMs) to provide a standardized, objective, and efficient grading system that could handle nuances in text and provide constructive feedback.

Goals/Objectives: The primary objectives were to:

  • Automate the grading process for text content.
  • Ensure consistency and objectivity in grading, reducing human bias.
  • Provide detailed feedback based on predefined rubrics.
  • Improve efficiency in evaluating large datasets of text.

My Involvement: My role in this project was multifaceted, involving research, model selection, prompt engineering, and system integration. I was responsible for exploring various LLMs, designing the grading rubrics in a machine-readable format, and crafting effective prompts to guide the LLM in its evaluation. I also worked on integrating the LLM's output into a user-friendly interface for reviewing grades and feedback.

Approach: The project followed a research and development approach:

  • LLM Selection: Researching and selecting the most suitable LLM based on its capabilities in natural language understanding and generation.
  • Rubric Definition: Collaborating with subject matter experts to define clear, measurable grading rubrics.
  • Prompt Engineering: Iteratively refining prompts to ensure the LLM understood the grading criteria and provided accurate assessments.
  • Feedback Generation: Designing the system to extract specific feedback points from the LLM's analysis.
  • Evaluation & Refinement: Comparing LLM-generated grades with human grades to fine-tune the system and improve accuracy.

Outcomes: The standardized grading system successfully demonstrated the potential of LLMs in automating content evaluation. It significantly reduced the time and effort required for grading, while also improving consistency across assessments. The system was able to provide detailed, rubric-based feedback, which proved valuable for content creators looking to improve their writing. This project highlighted my ability to apply cutting-edge AI technologies to solve real-world problems and optimize workflows.
